JSermonEdit

Editing Shortcuts:

Alt+Left/Right to promote (make headings) or demote (make bullet points).

Tab or Space at the beginning of a line will also make bullet points (even on mobile).

Shift+Alt+Left/Right to collapse/reveal all non-heading content.

Alt+Up/Down to move the selected lines and paragraphs up or down, swapping with lines and paragraphs in between. If the content of a section is collapsed, its children will move with their heading.

Shift+Alt+Up/Down to move a heading, paragraph, or line and all of its children.

Alt+H to hide/unhide the selected content. Hidden content doesn't count towards word counts.

Hiding a line hides its children.

This child line will be hidden if its parent is hidden.

Alt+Enter to toggle the appearance of selected content:

Bullet points become numbered.

Paragraphs are enlarged.

Shift+Alt+Enter to toggle other changes to the selected content:

Bullet points get a check mark.

Even if they're numbered.

Paragraphs are centered.

Alt+Backspace to delete the selected lines, headings, and paragraphs.

Ctrl+Z to Undo.

Ctrl+Shift+Z to Redo.

Saving and Loading:

Ctrl+S to Save your work.

Shift+Ctrl+S to Save As a different file.

Ctrl+O to Open a file.

Alt+S to Export in another format:

By default, JSermonEdit will be in "content" mode, just saving the content within the document.

But Alt+S will let you save the whole page -- "with editor", so that someone can open the file in their browser and see it properly formatted (and even continue editing it themselves!).

Opening a "with editor" file means saving will be "with editor" by default. So if you want to save just the content within the document, Alt+S will save a "content" file.

By default, JSermonEdit will be in "content" mode, just saving the content within the document. But 

The content within the document is saved as HTML. It needs to be opened by JSermonEdit to be displayed correctly.

Saving again will overwrite the same file.

Alt+N to start a new session, showing these tips again.
